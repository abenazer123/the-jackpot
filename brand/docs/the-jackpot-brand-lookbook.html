02 — The Story

How we got here

The Jackpot started with a house. A big, beautiful, carefully designed house on Chicago's North Side — 5 bedrooms, 3 bathrooms, space for 14. A hot tub under a louvered pergola. A 120-inch cinema with a popcorn machine. A game room with a bar, a poker table, and a record player. A fire pit surrounded by Adirondack chairs.

The interior was designed by Carla Schuessler under the vision "Chicago Modern Deco Revival" — warm cognac tones, sage greens, brass fixtures, a sputnik chandelier that catches light like a starburst. The kind of space where every room feels intentional and every photo turns out beautiful.

The brand needed to match. Not a generic rental logo. Not a sterile hospitality template. Something that captures the feeling of walking through the door and realizing you found something better than you expected. Something that makes people screenshot and send to the group chat. Something that glows.

03 — The Feeling

Fruit bowl and brunch

The brand lives in a specific emotional territory — warm, bright, celebratory, and alive. Never dark. Never cold. Never stiff.

Fruit bowl
Peach, mango, honey, citrus, mint. Fresh and abundant. Colors you want to eat. The warmth of things that grew in sunlight.
Brunch
Champagne in sunlight. Linen tablecloth. Friends laughing over eggs and coffee. The golden glow of a late morning when nobody's in a rush.
Golden hour
That 30 minutes before sunset when everything looks perfect, every photo turns out good, and the whole world looks warm.
Sunday morning
Windows open. Coffee made. Nowhere to be. Everyone still gathered in the living room, replaying last night. The afterglow of a great time.
"You found something better than you expected, and everyone's going to ask you how you found it."

08 — The Starburst

The motif

Geometric sunburst with gold lines and peach-tipped edges. Half sputnik chandelier, half neon bleed. The mark you'll see embedded in everything from the wordmark to the patterns to the watermarks behind the text.

Origin
Sputnik chandelier
The starburst is a direct callout to the brass sputnik chandelier hanging in the dining room. Every gathering happens under it. Every photo includes it.
Heritage
Chicago deco revival
The geometric burst nods to the city's Art Deco buildings — the radial sunbursts on the Carbide and Carbon, the angled ornament on the Chicago Board of Trade.
Color
Neon bleed
Gold lines with peach tips. The color shift mimics the bloom of a neon sign in a window — the moment the tube transitions from gas to glow.
Scale
Three intensities
100% as a primary mark. 30% as a divider or accent. 5% as a watermark or background texture. Same shape, different volumes.

11 — Physical Touchpoints

In the space

The brand isn't just digital. These are the small physical moments where the system shows up in the house itself.

✦
The neon sign
A small custom neon sign by the bar — the only place the brand wordmark appears in actual neon. Peach tubes, gold backing, the visual source of the "neon bleed" gradient.
⛁
Greenery wall
A vertical sage planter wall behind the dining table. Doubles as the photo backdrop everyone uses for the group shot. The actual sage color the brand pulls from.
✉
Welcome cards
Letterpress cards on linen stock waiting on the dining table. JACKPOT in deboss, "you found something special" in gold foil italics, the wifi password printed on the back.
❋
Coasters & guest book
Linen coasters with the starburst foil-stamped in gold. A leather-bound guest book on the bar with a pen tied to it. Everyone signs. Everyone reads.
12 — The Rules

Always and never

The non-negotiables. Everything in this lookbook lives inside these guardrails.

Always
  • Cormorant Garamond for display
  • Outfit for body and function
  • Warm olive #7a6030 for body text
  • Brand gradient at exactly 135°
  • Twinkle accents above and right of marks
  • Linen and warm tones as backgrounds
  • Smart curly quotes and em dashes
  • Star bullets (✦) in gold
  • Photos lit warm, never cool
Never
  • Black, dark gray, or espresso anywhere
  • JACKPOT in a dark color
  • JACKPOT on a dark background
  • Cool tones — no blue, gray, silver
  • Generic sans like Inter, Roboto, Arial
  • Gambling imagery — no dice, no chips
  • Pink (it's peach, not pink)
  • Dark scrim overlays on photos
  • The word "rental" in marketing copy
